Biodiversity Champions

Launched during the eleventh meeting of the Conference of the Parties to the Convention on Biological Diversity (COP 11), the Hyderabad Call for Biodiversity Champions aims to highlight Parties' commitment to biodiversity and to encourage all stakeholders to commit resources towards biodiversity in the spirit of Decision XI/4.

India became the first Biodiversity Champion during the COP 11 High-level Segment, when India’s Prime Minister announced the earmarking of US$50 million towards strengthening institutional mechanisms, enhancing technical and human capabilities for biodiversity conservation in India, and promoting similar capacity building in other developing countries during India's COP Presidency.

The Champions campaign seeks to highlight truly inventive, creative and exceptional examples of support to the Strategic Plan for Biodiversity 2011-2020. Its potential strength comes from the unique and memorable nature of the pledges, and the commitments to implementation that they bring.

Like the Government of India, and soon many others, you can become a Biodiversity Champion. Your pledge should be an exceptional contribution and initiative in support of the Aichi Biodiversity Targets, which can be used to inspire further action. Your pledge can:

  • Support one or more Aichi Biodiversity Targets
  • Cover a particular region or sub-region
  • Include financial, technical, or other forms of support
  • Be time-bound
